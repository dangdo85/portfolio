About Me

I have been a high school math teacher for the last 10 years and I am transitioning into the tech field, software development to be exact. Each teaching year is unique with a new group of over 100 students and parents, new content team members or administrators, and changes in the curriculum and policies. All this to say that I am familiar with an ever changing and evolving role. Being an educator has been challenging and fulfilling, but recent policies have removed accountability from students and left myself and other educators feeling ineffective. This motivated me to look into becoming a software developer.

My solution oriented approach, problem solving skill, and understanding how to teach and learn will greatly help my journey into this exciting career. Instead of vacationing this summer, I joined General Assembly’s software engineering immersive program and have now completed the 24 weeks, 450 plus hours, focusing on product development fundamentals, object-oriented programming, MVC frameworks, data modeling, and team collaboration strategies. My experience through the program has brought new excitement and energy to my workday. I enjoyed learning new technology that shapes our everyday life. The first time seeing my codes coming to life on the screen was a magical moment that fuels my desire to be a part of developing effective and easy to use programs to help meet people’s daily needs.

My newly found love for software development is growing as I continue to learn. I am looking for a position on a team that would support my budding passion with a healthy learning environment. I am eager to continue my journey to grow and to meet the needs of tech companies.
